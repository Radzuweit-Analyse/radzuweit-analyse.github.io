## Q4 2025 holds roughly steady at 5.2% growth

Q4 2025 barely moved the needle: the rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted growth rate held at 5.16%, essentially unchanged from 5.21% the previous quarter. Computed directly from the KPOKPCH tensor, as always; this quarter's own figure remains provisional and could still move.

### Scorecard: how did our forecast do?

As of the Q3 2025 vintage, our model projected 4.2% for Q4 2025 (80% band 2.0% to 6.8%). The actual, 5.16%, cleared that band's midpoint by a wide margin but still landed inside it, a hit on the high side.

![Annualized forecast fan chart, Q4 2025 vintage](/figures/forecast_ch_hairlines_annualized_yoy_2025Q4.png)

### Under the surface

The cantonal gap stretched further this quarter: Nidwalden again led, Vaud trailed, a gap of 12 points, nearly double the 6 points of a year earlier. Of the cost groups, SPITEX (home care) remained the fastest-growing; medication and long-term care sat at the other end, both among the lowest in the panel. Outpatient hospital care (Hôpitaux ambulatoire) was this quarter's biggest accelerator, a smaller swing than the last two quarters' movers and consistent with the quarter's overall flatness at the CH level.

### Refreshed outlook

4 quarters ahead, the outlook sits at 3.6% for Q4 2026 (80% band 0.8% to 7.7%), again well below the current quarter's own growth rate. That is the model's mean-reversion at work again, tugging the forecast back toward trend.
