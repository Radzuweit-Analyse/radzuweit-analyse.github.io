## Swiss health costs grew 5.2% in Q4 2025, holding roughly steady

Rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted cost per insured rose 5.16% year on year through Q4 2025, essentially unchanged from 5.21% the previous quarter. This is computed directly from the KPOKPCH tensor built on the FOPH/BAG MOKKE cost-monitoring data. As with every quarter in this series, the freshest print is provisional and can still revise.

### Scorecard: how did our forecast do?

As of the Q3 2025 vintage, our model projected 4.2% for Q4 2025 (80% band 2.0% to 6.8%). The actual, 5.16%, lands inside that band: a hit, though on the high side of the range.

![Annualized forecast fan chart, Q4 2025 vintage](/figures/forecast_ch_hairlines_annualized_yoy_2025Q4.png)

### Under the surface

Cantonal dispersion widened further. Nidwalden again led at 13.62%, while Vaud brought up the rear at 2.09%, an 11.53 point spread, nearly double the 6.35 point spread of a year earlier. SPITEX (home care) remained the fastest-growing of our 11 cost groups at 13.00%, with the residual "Autres" group next at 9.26%. Medication and long-term-care costs were the slowest movers, both under 4%. Outpatient hospital care (Hôpitaux ambulatoire) was this quarter's biggest accelerator, gaining 0.95 percentage points on its own growth rate, a smaller swing than the last two quarters' movers, consistent with the quarter's overall flatness at the CH level.

### Refreshed outlook

Looking 4 quarters ahead from this vintage, the model projects 3.6% by Q4 2026 (80% band 0.8% to 7.7%), again well below the current quarter's own growth rate. That is the same mean-reverting pattern this series has flagged before, not a directional call.
