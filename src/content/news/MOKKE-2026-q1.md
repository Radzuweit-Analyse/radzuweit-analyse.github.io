## A sharp deceleration: growth slows to 2.8% in Q1 2026

Rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted cost per insured rose 2.8% year on year through Q1 2026, down from 5.2% the previous quarter, a 2.4-point deceleration and the largest quarter-on-quarter swing in the headline figure since this series began. We use the rolling-4-quarter view because single-quarter comparisons are noisy here: Swiss OKP costs reset with deductibles each January and build through the year. Even on this smoothed measure, the size of the move stands out.

### Scorecard: how did our forecast do?

Our model's Q4 2025 vintage forecast for Q1 2026 was 4.8% (80% band 2.6% to 7.4%). The actual, 2.8%, barely cleared the band's lower edge. A hit, but a near miss.

![Annualized forecast fan chart, Q1 2026 vintage](/figures/forecast_ch_hairlines_annualized_yoy_2026Q1.png)

### Under the surface

Cantonal dispersion narrowed. Nidwalden again led, and Basel-Stadt became the only canton to post a year-on-year decline; the gap between them was 8 points, versus 6 a year earlier. SPITEX (home care) posted the fastest growth among cost groups; outpatient hospital care (Hôpitaux ambulatoire) was the only one to shrink outright, the main drag on this quarter's headline number.

Canton Zug's own hospital-inpatient costs (Hôpitaux séjours) dropped by more than 40% quarter on quarter in the raw data, from CHF 180 to CHF 105 per insured. The canton began paying hospital stays directly, outside the OKP channel, this quarter. That is a real, already-observed accounting change in this vintage's data.

### Refreshed outlook

The outlook now extends 4 quarters to Q1 2027: 7.6% (80% band 4.5% to 11.7%), a sharp rebound from this quarter's own 2.8%. That comes from the model's mean-reverting dynamics pulling back toward the sample's long-run growth rate, not a forecast that the current slowdown reverses on its own.
