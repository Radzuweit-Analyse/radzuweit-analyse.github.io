## Swiss health costs grew +2.8% in Q1 2026, a sharp deceleration

Rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted cost per insured rose **2.80%** year-on-year through Q1 2026, down from **+5.16%** the previous quarter -- a 2.35-percentage-point deceleration, the largest quarter-on-quarter swing in the headline figure since this series started tracking it. We use the rolling-4-quarter view specifically because single-quarter comparisons are noisy for this data (Swiss OKP costs reset with deductibles each January and build through the year); the size of this move nonetheless stands out even on the smoothed measure.

### Scorecard: how did our forecast do?

As of the Q4 2025 vintage, our model projected **+5.31%** for Q1 2026 (80% band +3.66% to +7.24%). The actual, +2.80%, falls below that entire band -- a miss, the first of this series. We'd rather report that plainly than explain it away: a deceleration of this size was outside what the model, built on parameter estimates from 41 quarterly observations, considered plausible a quarter out. Our [methodology article](/articles/kpopkpch_forecasting_article) is upfront that these bands reflect innovation noise only, not full parameter uncertainty -- a miss like this is exactly the kind of event that limitation predicts will happen more often than the nominal 80% coverage suggests.

### Under the surface

- **Cantonal dispersion narrowed**: Nidwalden again led at +7.23%, while Basel-Stadt was the only canton to post a year-on-year decline, at -0.75% -- a 7.97-point spread, versus 5.69 points a year earlier.
- **SPITEX (home care) remained the fastest-growing of our 11 cost groups** at +13.95%, followed by physiotherapy at +6.96%; outpatient hospital care (Hôpitaux ambulatoire) was the only group to shrink outright, at -4.11%, the main drag on this quarter's headline number.
- **Canton Zug's own hospital-inpatient costs (Hôpitaux séjours) dropped by more than 40% quarter-on-quarter in the raw data** (from CHF 179.50 to CHF 105.03 per insured), coinciding with the canton's move to pay hospital stays directly rather than through OKP starting this quarter -- a real, already-observed accounting change in this vintage's data, not a model assumption.

### Refreshed outlook

Looking 4 quarters ahead from this vintage, the model projects **+7.57%** by Q1 2027 (80% band +4.49% to +11.74%) -- a sharp rebound from this quarter's own +2.80%, driven by the model's mean-reverting dynamics pulling back toward the sample's long-run growth rate rather than a forecast that the current slowdown will reverse on its own.
