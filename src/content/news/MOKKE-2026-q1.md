## Swiss health costs grew 2.8% in Q1 2026, a sharp deceleration

Rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted cost per insured rose 2.80% year on year through Q1 2026, down from 5.16% the previous quarter. That is a 2.35 percentage point deceleration, the largest quarter-on-quarter swing in the headline figure since this series started tracking it. We use the rolling-4-quarter view because single-quarter comparisons are noisy for this data (Swiss OKP costs reset with deductibles each January and build through the year), and the size of this move stands out even on the smoothed measure.

### Scorecard: how did our forecast do?

As of the Q4 2025 vintage, our model projected 4.8% for Q1 2026 (80% band 2.6% to 7.4%). The actual, 2.80%, lands just inside that band, near its lower edge, so this scores as a hit.

### Under the surface

Cantonal dispersion narrowed. Nidwalden again led at 7.23%, while Basel-Stadt was the only canton to post a year-on-year decline, at -0.75%, a 7.97 point spread versus 5.69 points a year earlier. SPITEX (home care) remained the fastest-growing of our 11 cost groups at 13.95%, followed by physiotherapy at 6.96%. Outpatient hospital care (Hôpitaux ambulatoire) was the only group to shrink outright, at -4.11%, the main drag on this quarter's headline number. Canton Zug's own hospital-inpatient costs (Hôpitaux séjours) dropped by more than 40% quarter on quarter in the raw data, from CHF 179.50 to CHF 105.03 per insured, coinciding with the canton's move to pay hospital stays directly rather than through OKP starting this quarter. That is a real, already-observed accounting change in this vintage's data, not a model assumption.

### Refreshed outlook

Looking 4 quarters ahead from this vintage, the model projects 7.6% by Q1 2027 (80% band 4.5% to 11.7%), a sharp rebound from this quarter's own 2.80%. That is driven by the model's mean-reverting dynamics pulling back toward the sample's long-run growth rate, not a forecast that the current slowdown reverses on its own.
