## Accelerating again: Q3 2025 growth hits 5.2%

The rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted growth rate accelerated again in Q3 2025, to 5.2%, up from 4.5% the previous quarter, a 0.7-point rise. Our own computation, from the KPOKPCH tensor; the current quarter's figure is provisional and subject to revision, as always in this series.

### Scorecard: how did our backtest do?

Fit using only data through Q2 2025, a rolling-origin backtest projected 4.4% for Q3 2025 (80% band 2.4% to 7.1%). The actual, 5.2%, landed inside that band but well toward its upper end. Call it a hit that undersold the quarter's acceleration.

![Annualized forecast fan chart, Q3 2025 vintage](/figures/forecast_ch_hairlines_annualized_yoy_2025Q3.png)

### Under the surface

Cantonal dispersion widened sharply: Nidwalden led, Glarus barely moved, a gap of 13 points, well above the 10 points of a year earlier. Among cost groups, SPITEX (home care) and "Autres" again led, while medication costs and hospital inpatient stays (Hôpitaux séjours) tied for the slowest. Pharmacies were this quarter's biggest accelerator, a change of pace from the "Autres" group that led the prior two quarters.

### Refreshed outlook

Extended 4 quarters, the outlook drops to 3.6% by Q3 2026 (80% band 0.9% to 7.5%), well under this quarter's own 5.2%. That gap reflects the model's mean-reverting dynamics pulling the forecast back toward trend.
