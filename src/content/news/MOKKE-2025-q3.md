## Swiss health costs grew 5.2% in Q3 2025, accelerating again

Rolling-4-quarter, population-weighted cost per insured rose 5.21% year on year through Q3 2025, up from 4.47% the previous quarter, a 0.74 percentage point acceleration. This is our own computation from the KPOKPCH tensor. The freshest print in any quarter is provisional and can still revise.

### Scorecard: how did our backtest do?

A rolling-origin backtest fit using only data through Q2 2025 projected 4.4% for Q3 2025 (80% band 2.4% to 7.1%). The actual, 5.21%, lands inside that band but toward its upper end: a hit, though the model underestimated just how much growth would accelerate this quarter.

![Annualized forecast fan chart, Q3 2025 vintage](/figures/forecast_ch_hairlines_annualized_yoy_2025Q3.png)

### Under the surface

Cantonal dispersion widened sharply. Nidwalden led at 14.28%, Glarus barely moved at 0.80%, a 13.48 point spread, well above the 9.56 point spread a year earlier. SPITEX (home care) and "Autres" again led our 11 cost groups, at 12.53% and 12.37%, while medication costs and hospital inpatient stays (Hôpitaux séjours) tied for the slowest at 3.76%. Pharmacies were this quarter's biggest accelerator, gaining 2.00 percentage points on their own growth rate, a change of pace from the "Autres" group that led the prior two quarters.

### Refreshed outlook

Looking 4 quarters ahead from this vintage, the model projects 3.6% by Q3 2026 (80% band 0.9% to 7.5%), notably below this quarter's own 5.21%. That reflects the model's mean-reverting dynamics rather than a call that growth is about to reverse abruptly.
